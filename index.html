On April 1st and 2nd women developers from all different skill levels and backgrounds will get together to learn and hack on projects for local non-profits and minority-owned businesses.

Projects include web sites for disaster response, youth empowerment, women's health, sex worker rights, immigration, and journalism.

Saturday, April 2, 2016
8am to 6pm
Enova International, 175 W. Jackson Blvd., Chicago, IL

Installfest

Friday, April 1, 2016 from 5pm to 9pm

Childcare available.

Chicago Women Developers Girl Develop It Chicago PyLadies Chicago RailsBridge Chicago Women Who Code Chicago Write/Speak/Code Chicago

invite you to participate in a hackathon for anyone who identifies as a woman or as non-binary.

Schedule

Saturday April 2nd, 8am-7pm

Arrival & Registration @ 8am
Breakfast @ 8:30

Working lunch @ 12pm
Snack @ 4.30
Demos @ 6
Wrap Up @ 7

>This event is open to anyone who identifies as a woman or as non-binary and is comfortable in a women-only space. Developers of all languages and skill levels are encouraged to participate. Prior to the event, we will be posting a code of conduct which all participants must agree and adhere to.

Registration opens in late February.
